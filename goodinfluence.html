Good Morals From Anime

Once people are invested in anime, there are many teachings and morals that they will likely pick up. One of the strongest themes taught to young children through anime is the power of friendship. People are raised to believe that they can overcome anything when working with their friends and family. This pushes them to create meaningful relationships with other people and maintain them during their life. Some strong pushers of this teaching are pet-based stories like Digimon and Pokémon. They show that the creatures and their masters must work together to defeat evil or accomplish their goals. Other teachings include an attempt at instilling confidence in one’s self and their abilities. They’ll show a kid with a dream. All the odds will be stacked against them from the beginning, but they’ll work hard putting in seemingly more effort than anyone else. Watchers will see the character’s efforts prove fruitful at times and more commonly fail. However, the main point being showcased is the character’s determination and confidence. When their efforts fail or seem to have been wasted, they do not fall to depression or indifference. They pick themselves back up and strive to work even harder. This theme is something that has been shown in many variations of media from other countries as well, but in anime, particularly Shōnen, it is conveyed strongly and often.
